# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We — the maintainers and contributors of djangordf — pledge to make
participation in this project a respectful, welcoming, and harassment-free
experience for everyone, regardless of background, identity, or
experience level. We commit to acting and interacting in ways that
build an open, diverse, and healthy community.

## Our standards

We expect every participant to:

- Treat one another with empathy, kindness, and patience.
- Give and accept constructive feedback in good faith.
- Acknowledge the perspectives and lived experience of others.
- Take responsibility for mistakes, apologise to those affected, and
  use the experience to learn.
- Prioritise what is best for the overall community, not just for
  individual contributors.

Conduct that demeans, threatens, intimidates, or marginalises another
participant is not acceptable in any project space — including this
repository, its issue tracker, pull requests, the documentation
site, and any other forum where djangordf is discussed under the
project's name.

This Code of Conduct adopts the spirit and intent of the
[Contributor Covenant, version 2.1](https://www.contributor-covenant.org/version/2/1/code_of_conduct/),
without reproducing the full text. Where this document is silent or
ambiguous, the maintainers will defer to the Covenant's published
guidance.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces and also when an
individual is officially representing the project in public spaces
(for example, using an official email address, posting from an
official social media account, or acting as an appointed
representative at an online or offline event).

## Reporting

Instances of behaviour that may violate this Code can be reported to
the maintainer at **benjamin.schnabel@ephe.psl.eu**. Reports will be
reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. The maintainer is
obliged to respect the privacy and safety of the reporter.

## Enforcement

Maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing standards
of acceptable behaviour and will take appropriate, proportionate
corrective action in response to any behaviour they consider in
violation of this Code. Actions may include private warnings,
temporary or permanent removal of comments, commits, or pull
requests, and — in serious or repeated cases — a temporary or
permanent ban from the project.

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce the Code of Conduct in good
faith may face temporary or permanent repercussions as determined by
other members of the project's leadership.
